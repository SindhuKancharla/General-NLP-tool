Panaji: More bodies were retrieved from the debris of the collapsed building in Canacona town of Goa on Sunday night, taking the death toll to 17, while over a dozen more people are still feared trapped in the debris.
Rescue operation by Army personnel and local civic agencies, using earth-moving machinery, continued without a pause for a second night on Sunday after the building collapsed on the afternoon of 4 January.
A senior fire and emergency services officer at the site told PTI that one body was recovered late last night and another at midnight, taking the toll to 17. Sixteen people have been admitted to state-run Hospicio Hospital in Margao and Goa Medical College and Hospital near here. Rescuers said 15 more people are still trapped in the debris.
The five-storey building was coming up in Chawdi area of Canacona town, 70 km from here. Police have already registered a case against the builder Vishwas Desai and contractor Jaideep Saigal. Both are untraceable, police said.
