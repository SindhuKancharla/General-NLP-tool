14 killed, 18 trapped in Canacona building collapse

Karsten Miranda & Ashokkumar Desai, TNN Jan 5, 2014, 01.47AM IST

  • Manohar Parrikar|

CANACONA: Little did they realize they were having the last meal of their life. Sitting in the shade of the building they were engaged to construct, 14 labourers died while 28 others were rescued after the five-storey structure came crashing down on Saturday afternoon in Chaudi, Canacona. Unofficial figures put a higher death toll.

Among the dead are locals Vikas Deu Velip and Sudhakar Ganesh Velip, both residents of Canacona, who were working at the site. Most of the 50-odd labourers who were at the site at the time of the incident are from Odisha and Karnataka.

Till going to the press, over 18 persons were feared trapped, even as rescue operations were still under way. Authorities fear the death toll is likely to increase as rescuers said they could hear victims crying from under the debris. Following a request from the state government, two units of the Indian Army—one each from Margao and Bambolim—joined in the rescue operations. Heavy earthmoving machinery is also being used. The National Disaster Management Response Force from Pune is expected to arrive late night to join in the rescue.

While three blocks of the project—entirely six buildings and 150 flats—are ready and even have flats occupied, the remaining portion is under construction. The structure that collapsed had 24 flats and was one of the three incomplete buildings. Among those who own apartments in the project are actor Asha Parekh, sports minister Ramesh Tawadkar and some babus.

Venancio Furtado, acting South Goa collector told the media that the under-construction building was being built by Ruby Constructions of Bharat Developers, a Mumbai-based company. South Goa SP Shekar Prabhudesai said a case under IPC Sections 304 and 304A has been registered against builders Jugdeep Kumar Sehgal and Pradeep Singh, both partners, director Vishwas Desai, and others, for negligence.

Chief minister Manohar Parrikar, who reached the spot around 4.30pm, said prima facie the collapse was due to poor quality of construction. "We checked the nearby blocks of the project, and even they had developed cracks. The district administration will look into all aspects of the case and submit a report to the government." He added that action will also be taken against officials of the town and country planning department if they are found guilty in the inquiry report. The RCC consultants of the project too will be booked.

A pall of fear and gloom spread through Canacona as the collapse triggered shock waves. The project is located in the heart of the town and with Saturday also being bazaar day there were nearly 3,000 locals in the market, barely 500m from the site. On hearing the "fearsome crash sounds" and cries for help, residents and passers-by tried to intervene. But as the trapped were buried deep inside the debris—most of the slabs had come crashing down—any rescue effort had to wait till the first fire tender arrived from the Canacona fire station. Fire tenders and personnel from Sanvordem and Margao also rushed to the site.

Those rescued from the debris were rushed to the Canacona community health centre and Hospsicio Hospital, Margao. One victim was sent to the Goa medical college and hospital, Bambolim.

Sources said 400 policemen were posted at the site as over 2,000 people gathered and tension built up with locals demanding the arrest of the builder and others connected with the firm. More than 400 flats, apartments and shops are under construction by Bharat Developers as the firm has taken up projects in the Canacona municipality areas of Chaudi, Nagarcem and Devabhag.

Eyewitnesses said many of the labourers had been engaged in work, including plumbing, on the floors of the building. Around eight security guards were also seen, eyewitnesses claimed. Labourers who were at the three-day, government-organized Lokutsav, which began on Friday, had a lucky escape, as did a family of labourers and other workers who were resting outside the building and were having their lunch elsewhere.

Early into the rescue operation, a labourer in a white shirt was seen atop the collapsed debris crying for help as his leg was trapped. At ground level, the bleeding head of a labourer could be seen from under the debris. "Some of the rescued persons have been admitted to a local hospital with minor injuries," said Canacona fire station in-charge Namdev Parwar.

Canacona municipal council (CMC) chairperson Mahadev Desai said the council will conduct an inquiry into the incident. He alleged a nexus between the TCP and builders, stating that the state officials should have checked for the stability of the structure. "It is very unfortunate that the incident has occurred," said Desai.

Prashant Shirodkar, deputy collector and CMC chief officer, said the municipality had issued the construction license but not the occupancy certificate as the building was still under construction.

Times View

The warning bells have been there with rampant construction being allowed by authorities with little checks and balances. Permissions are being given to construct multi-storied buildings all over the state and such a disaster was waiting to happen. Though the first in the state, it is time the government carries out an inspection of all construction under progress and review the safety of buildings. Use of poor quality material and used material in construction has led to several collapses in Mumbai and Goa should learn its lessons from those tragedies and put in place appropriate measures.
